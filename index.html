About

SIQUIJOR

Siquijor Island is a small but enchanting island in the Philippines, often called the “Mystic Island” because of its long history of healers, folklore, and mystical traditions. The island is rich in natural beauty, with pristine beaches, waterfalls, caves, and lush forests that attract both locals and tourists. During the Spanish era, it was named “Isla del Fuego” (Island of Fire) due to the glowing fireflies seen by explorers. Siquijor also preserves its cultural heritage through historic churches like Lazi Church and centuries-old trees such as the Balete Tree, where visitors can even experience a natural fish spa. Today, the island offers a perfect mix of relaxation, adventure, and cultural exploration, making it a unique destination in the Philippines.

Trivia about Siquijor Island

Interesting

Basic knowledge about the Island of Siquijor in Bohol Sea, southeast of Negros Island

Mystic Island

It is famous in the Philippines for stories about traditional healers, folk magic, and mystical practices.

Island of Fire

During the Spanish period, the island was called “Isla del Fuego” (Island of Fire) because Spanish explorers saw thousands of glowing fireflies in the trees.

one of the smallest provinces

Siquijor is one of the smallest provinces in the Philippines, but it is rich in natural attractions like beaches, waterfalls, and caves.

Cambugahay Falls

The island is home to the famous Cambugahay Falls, a three-level waterfall known for its turquoise water and rope swings.

Balete Tree

One of the oldest and most unique trees in the country is the Balete Tree, which is over 400 years old and has a natural fish spa at its base.

Healing Festival

Every year, locals hold the Healing Festival during Holy Week where traditional healers gather to prepare herbal medicines.
